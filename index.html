About Me

I started at UC Irvine where I earned my BS in Physics with a specialization in condensed matter physics. I was in Professor Jing Xia’s group from 5/2017 – 9/2018 focusing on experimental condensed matter physics.

In my project I synthesized crystals, spot welded and soldered, prepared experiments in a dilution fridge, and analyzed data with the goal of better understanding topological insulators for building quantum computers.

There I developed and established my career dream to study theoretical condensed matter physics and chemistry through simulations and data analysis.

I joined Professor Kieron Burke’s group in the Physics and Chemistry Department from 1/2019 – 2/2020. I performed calculations on diamagnetic molecules computing electric field gradients, nuclear magnetic resonance chemical shifts, and magnetic anisotropies using density functional theory.

I analyzed the accuracy of density functional theory to predict nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) properties using different functionals and pseudopotentials. I used spreadsheets, scripts, and statistical analysis to determine accuracies and explored ways to improve NMR predictions.

I started at UC Merced in Fall 2021 and have been in Professor Christine Isborn’s group since then.
